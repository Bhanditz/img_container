US Poker Rooms: The Best Legal Online Poker Sites in the United States

There are many US poker rooms offering online games, with unfortunately many of them offering grey market services. While we recommend you to avoid these sites, the good news if you are living in the United States is that you can still play legally with wide variety of US eligible poker sites.

US online poker players that live in or plan to visit the states of Delaware, Nevada, and New Jersey, can legally play US poker online at a variety of state-regulated US poker money sites with some of the best software around.

Poker players can still play in US poker online in most other states, where subscription based poker in most cases is fully legal.

How to find the best legal US poker site?

With so many legal US online poker companies, how easy is it to find the right one for you?

If you don’t live in, or plan to visit the states of Delaware, Nevada, and New Jersey, you should look into subscription-based online poker as this is your only legal option.

The software on subscription based sites is easy to use whether you have played poker online or not. Since the software was designed with the recreational player in mind, it is often web-based and not conducive to multi-tabling.

The games are not only fun due to the casual setting, but you can often have opportunities for big prizes including packages to take part in land-based poker events, and cold hard cash.

The best part of subscription-based sites is that there really is no risk, since you can play in lots of events for free when becoming a paid member of a site. These sites include also games available to players who want to enjoy online poker without becoming a paid member.

The states of Nevada, Delaware, and New Jersey all have passed regulations allowing regulated online poker within their borders. You should consider checking them out if you live near one or plan to visit one of the States in the list, as you do not need to be a resident of the state to play poker for real money, but merely present in the state while the games take place.

US Online Poker: are these sites really legal?

The Unlawful Internet Gaming Enforcement Act (UIGEA) of 2006 changed the landscape by which online poker operators could legally operate, specifically prohibiting "gambling businesses from knowingly accepting payments in connection with the participation of another person in a bet or wager that involves the use of the Internet and that is unlawful under any federal or state law.”

Subscription based sites in the US are not covered in this US law since there is no entry fee for any of the tournaments or ring games. While widely legal throughout the United States, some states in the union do prohibit play on subscription based poker rooms.

State-regulated sites are fully legal and exempt from the UIGEA since the legislation contains a carve-out for legal intrastate gambling. All of the state regulated sites on this page have been licensed by the state’s gaming authority and are legal for anyone of legal age to play at for real money as long as they are in the state’s borders.
