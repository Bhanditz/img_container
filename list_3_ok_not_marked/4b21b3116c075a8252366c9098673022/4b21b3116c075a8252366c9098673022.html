Practicing Poker for Free
We will shortly be moving on to more specific lessons about how to play poker, but before you are even dealt any cards - much less see a flop, turn or river - you will need to know how to find a table and sit down.

There is no better way to learn poker than through hands-on experience. You will learn a good deal more if you are able to put the theory from these lessons into practice straight away.

The PokerStars software allows players to join the action without depositing any money. All players are given a balance of "play money" chips, which have no monetary value, but which allow beginners to learn the mechanics of the game in exactly the same fashion as if at a real money table.



Playing with play money chips at a play money table is a great way to develop your poker skills.

You can start playing free online poker, and practicing in a poker club, as soon as you have set up your PokerStars account. The play money tables are located under the "Play Money" tab in the PokerStars lobby. You then have the following options:
  • All
  • Fixed limit
  • No limit
  • Pot limit

The list of available tables will update when you click on one of the different options, and the poker tables can be opened by clicking on their names. If there is a seat available, you take it by clicking "Seat Open".

If you lose all your play money chips, you can top up with 12.500 chips for free every 4 hours." or more generic as "You can top up your chips for free every few hours.

One important thing to remember about play money tables is that some players can play very recklessly because no money is at stake. They might enter pots with any two cards.

Many of the strategy considerations we teach at PokerStars School may not be as effective at a play money table. Therefore, if you have been reading our articles and already have a fairly good grasp of how to play, you will probably benefit more from playing at PokerStars micro-stakes tables.

Play money tables are however ideal for absolute beginners to get familiar with the mechanics of poker: when to act, how to bet, call or fold, etc.

THE ACTION IS ON YOU
