Real Money Poker Variations

Now that we’ve made the first steps in choosing the real money poker site that you want to play at, it’s time to decide what game variation(s) you want to play.

Most sites offer a number of poker options. Among the most common are:

  • Texas Hold ’em (Limit, No Limit, Pot Limit)
  • Seven-Card Stud
  • Five-Card Draw
  • Five-Card Stud
  • Omaha (High and High/Low)

Texas Hold ‘em

In Texas hold ’em, the dealer doles out two cards to each play. These cards are placed face-down in front of the players. The players can look at these cards and the initial betting round begins.

Next, the dealer will place 3 cards in center of the table face-up. This is known as the flop.

After the flop, another round of betting begins. The goal is to get the best hand you can with five of the eventual seven cards that will be dealt.

This is followed by the fourth cards (known as the turn) and the final card, or the river card. There is betting before and after the final card. In each round of betting, you can decide to check, call, raise or fold.

Seven Card Stud

In seven card stud, each player is dealt two cards face down and a third card face up. The betting starts with the player with the lowest ranking card facing up and then proceeds clockwise. Going forward the dealer will deal one card to each player at a time with a betting round to follow.

Betting starts with the best hand showing, then goes clockwise. After the 3rd card, the next 3 cards are dealt face up then the final card face up for a total of 7 cards. After the 5th betting round, a showdown occurs (assuming there are still at least two players left in the game), where each player left in the game chooses the best five cards to make the best hand.

Five Card Draw

Five card draw is probably the most familiar game outside of Texas H\hold ’em. The initial betting starts with the player to the left of the dealer. The dealer then deals all five cards to each player face down, which the player can look at.

This is followed by a round of betting. Next, players are then given the option of discarding and requesting new cards. Another round of betting then occurs. Then a second round of betting, then the showdown. The player with the best hand wins the pot.

Five Card Stud

Five card stud is another popular poker variant. In this game one card is dealt face down and the other four are face up. Betting starts after the second card is dealt. The player with the lowest card starts the betting. Betting subsequently occurs after the third, fourth and fifth cards with the starting bet in each round coming from the player with the best hand showing. The winner is the person with the best hand after the fifth card.

Omaha (High and High/Low)

For the more adventurous players, Omaha is popular. Several variants are offered at different real money poker sites, the most popular of which are Omaha high and Omaha high/low

Because Omaha is a hold ’em game, there is a community set of cards with which the players will need to develop their hand with. Players are dealt four cards face down and five community cards. The player must use only two of the face down cards he is dealt along with three of the community cards as part of his hand.

In Omaha High, it works more like Texas hold ’em but the player must make the best hand from his 2 down cards and three community cards. In high/low version the pot is split between the best high hand and the best low hand.
