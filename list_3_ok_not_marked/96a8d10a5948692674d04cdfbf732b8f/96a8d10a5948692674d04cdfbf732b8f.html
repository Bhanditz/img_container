Large Tournaments

The Largest Tournaments

Undoubtedly, the most prestigious and large poker events in the world are the World Series of Poker, also known as WSOP. They are held every year between May and July in Las Vegas. The $10,000 WSOP Main Event has established its status as the most important poker tournament in history and the largest challenge before poker pros. The prize pool of that Main Event will normally reach an eight-digit figure, where 2006 saw a colossal $82,512,162 given away.
Another popular tournament series is the World Poker Tour, also known as WPT, inspired by the WSOP and featuring a similar structure. The WPT World Championship, its Main Event, is in fact the second largest poker tournament in the world after the WSOP Main Event.
The largest event in Europe is definitely the European Poker Tour, also known as EPT, which includes events in some of the most beautiful European cities.

How can poker programs improve my gameplay?!

Poker programs get increasingly more popular and nowadays you may find a number of tools such as PokerTracker, that may help improve your gameplay. That program’s sole disadvantage is that it presents a complex mathematical concept really correctly, but it can hardly analyse your opponent’s behaviour. That is why it is recommendable to combine PokerTracker with ICM (that is, Independent Chip Model). That model calculates the estimated value (EV) of your profit according to your chip number. On that basis, it calculates whether an All-in can be won in the long run. At that, it considers the starting cards, the blinds, the payout structure as well as your opponent’s possible All-in hands. Of course, that range is hard to estimate, so the readings are not always 100% correct. The more you use such programs, the faster you will start taking your independent and intuitive decisions.
