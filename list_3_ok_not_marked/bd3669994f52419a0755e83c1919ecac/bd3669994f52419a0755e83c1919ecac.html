Best Free Poker Sites - Where to Play Poker Freerolls

Want to build an online poker bankroll with zero risk? Poker Freerolls are the answer. 

For those new to the poker game, a “freeroll” is a free poker tournament that doesn’t have an entry fee but pays the winner (or winners) in real money – or at least in a “real-money value” prize like a tournament-entry ticket or some poker merchandise.

Free poker tournaments come in all shapes and sizes, but typically offer a prize pool of a few hundred dollars. At any size, they’re by far the best way to build your bankroll from scratch and improve your game without risking any of your own money.

Freerolls are almost as old as online poker itself. Since the very early stages of the online poker industry there has been intense competition among online poker rooms to recruit new sign-ups.

About Free Poker Sites
Freerolls really are free.

One result of that competition was the invention of the "freerolls" or free poker tournament on several sites.

To put it simply: a freeroll is a poker tournament with no cost to enter but with a real-money or real-value prize.

Playing freeroll tournaments is the best way to sharpen your poker strategy skills without having to wager any money.

There are, however, many different kinds of freerolls and finding the best ones can be complicated for new poker players.

Below you'll find a guide that should answer some of your most burning freeroll questions:

 

What are Poker Freerolls?

A freeroll is a poker tournament with no entry fee.

The player is not on the hook for any money and is essentially playing free online poker.

Thse chips mean something.

How are Freerolls Different from a Free Poker Sites?

If you've played on any kind of "free play" poker app or social poker game like Zynga poker, that's a "free play" site.

There's no real money to be won and all of the chips in play are just token markers to keep score and bragging rights, more or less. You can win your free chips or buy more but they're not worth any "real" money per se.

A "freeroll" is held on a real online poker site where most players are playing for real money.

For this specific type of tournament, though, there is no "buy-in" required (other than occasionally your accrued "player points) you acquire by playing real-money games and tournaments).

So you can play in the event entirely for free. Which is cool. But even cooler is the payout is usually of real money value - either the form of actual cash or in a ticket to play in a tournament with a real-money buy-in.

Most poker sites will give new player who open new accounts a few freerolls to help boost their bankrolls when they get started. They'll also typically offer a free tournament tickets, added to your bankroll for free, as well.

Many professional players got their start playing in freeroll poker tournaments. They won a few bucks, reinvested those in more tournaments (or continued playing more freerolls and won again), and soon enough were on their way to a steady poker income.

Are Poker Freerolls Actually Free?

Yes, although there can be some minor restrictions.

Sometimes poker freerolls are limited to players who recently signed up for an online poker site while others are restricted to players who played a certain number of real-money poker hands in the previous month.

Of course there are some freerolls where you can qualify for just being from a certain region or even being a Facebook fan of a famous poker player.

In fact if you're on the lookout you'll find opportunities to play freerolls or free poker tournaments quite frequently.

All you need is a bit of due diligence and to pay attention to social media accounts for popular poker sites or poker TV shows like PokerStars and 888poker or EPT Live.

In reality there is free cash to be won and free poker tournaments for almost every type of poker player so be sure you have a look around and see where the best deals are.

Most players who sign up at a poker room via PokerListings are also typically eligible for exclusive weekly and monthly freerolls just for PokerListings.com players.

These usually range in value from $500 to up to $25,000 and PokerListings has paid out almost $7 million in freerolls since 2005.

Why Do Poker Sites Offer Freerolls?

Online poker rooms usually offer freerolls as a way to attract new players. It's also a way for rooms to build loyalty with existing players and better establish their brand.

In some ways it's similar to have cell phone or cable companies offer the first month free.

 

What Can I Expect to Win in Freerolls?

The prizes in freerolls can vary a great deal.

Prizes vary.

Sometimes there are small stakes freerolls that only award a few bucks while there have been several freerolls in the history of online poker that have awarded $1 million in free cash from the prize pool.

You can expect standard sign-up freeroll prize pools to be in the $500-$2,000 range.

How Do I Qualify for Freerolls?

Usually just by signing up for an online poker room.

Most poker sites also offer regular daily, weekly and monthly freerolls for new players or players who have made a deposit recently.

It's also possible to qualify by generating a certain amount of frequent player points on a site you already play on.

Some sites even offer freerolls for contest winners or becoming one of its Facebook fans/liking a social media post.
