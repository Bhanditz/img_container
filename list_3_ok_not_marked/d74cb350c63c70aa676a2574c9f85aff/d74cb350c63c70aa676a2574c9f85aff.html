Orangeburg mom wins $275,000 in lottery

  • T&D Staff Report
  • Dec 18, 2017
  • 0
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

An Orangeburg mother of two won $275,000 playing the lottery’s biggest ticket a week before Christmas, the S.C. Education Lottery Commission announced Monday. 

“I purchased the ticket and put it in my pocketbook,” said the winner. This ticket didn’t fit in her pocket.

The $10 ticket she won with is Royal Jewel Jackpot, one of the lottery’s new Gigantix tickets. They are over twice the size of a regular lottery ticket.

The winner plans to purchase a home for her family.

Circle K #5389 at 2744 North Road in Orangeburg sold the $275,000 winning ticket.

For selling the claimed ticket, Circle K #5389 in Orangeburg received a commission of $2,750.

    There have been several big lottery wins in The T&D Region in recent weeks.

    In late November, someone bought a $200,000 Palmetto Cash 5 ticket from the Palm Pantry #1 on Main Highway. A Palm Pantry #1 employee said someone hit a $250,000 win from a ticket sold the week before that.

    During the first week in December, the S.C. Education Lottery Commission announced a Bamberg man won $150,000. E-Z Shop #18 in Bamberg sold the ticket.

    Per-capita lottery spending in Orangeburg County is the highest in the state at $1,217, according to a study by the S.C. Commission on Minority Affairs.

    Next is at Jasper County at $1,035, followed by Bamberg County, $999; Clarendon County, $990, and Marlboro County, $943.
